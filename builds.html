F1Artifact

“Here & Now Together” — film treatment

The hero brand film one-pager: logline, the three acts, and two signatures — the ripple (when it happens) and the seam (how it looks: illustration resolving into photograph, bracketing the whole film — an opening dissolve, the mid-film seam, and a closing double-seam ring tag). Act I opens on an illustrated bubble-train before resolving into the live churn, plus "the mind movie"; an intention-setting scene shows all three canon reasons to look down in order — including a new matched pair of payoffs, "the magical serendipity of timing," where presence catches someone toward a yes and, in "the softening," catches someone else a half-second before they'd have said something sharp — bridging into "finding the others" and a substantially expanded climax — four match-cut vignettes widening the emotional range, the constellation, the film's only spoken dialogue — then a new closing "proof" beat (the ring exploded to reveal its vibrating element, the battery spec, a real-testimonials montage) before the tag, and the 30-second cutdown. Act II now also carries three real-testimonial overlays — brief, sourced captions woven under the ring-pulse, the focus-desk return, and the father-and-child return — seeding proof ahead of the closing montage rather than saving it all for one beat.

ST1Live

The Brand Strategy — the deck

The full positioning deck: the situation (digitally flooded, chronically braced — overstimulation as a measurable stress response, not a feeling), the one buyer (the Overstimulated Striver, one striver mapped across 8 moments), and the reframe the whole strategy hangs on — "we don't need more intention, we need better interruption." Positioning as the reset cue for real life: a screen-free cue to come back before stress takes over. One master promise, three pillars ("One pulse. One breath. One reset." · clear your mind without opening another app · turn reset into a habit), the brand operating system, and the go-to-market spine — don't sell the ring, sell the interruption; start with one buyer only; own the cultural moment.
