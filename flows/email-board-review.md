# The board reviews the email flows

*Advisory board — Alex Hormozi · Seth Godin · Alan Watts · Steve Jobs · Rory Sutherland. Evaluation of the 8 lifecycle flows / 55 emails, synthesized over 2 EBI passes.*

## The headline move
Fix the two things that betray the product: (1) strip every "score / grade / re-quiz / streak" so a ring that "knows nothing about you, on purpose" truly keeps no score of anyone, and (2) thread the board's unconditional 30-day return — currently in ZERO of the 55 emails — through the close and the arrival. Both move units and protect the anti-data soul without adding one number, countdown, or hype word.

## Flow report

### AWAKENING (quiz to purchase)
World-class nurture that opens on a grade and never closes — de-score the front, add the offer, cut the duplicates.

- Rename email-quiz-results off the banned word 'score'; its own hook already carries the fix ('a map, not a grade')
- Thread the unconditional 30-day return through email-when-youre-ready (keep the canon subject) and email-still-thinking, and give the flow one quiet buy ask — 11 emails currently make no clean offer
- Cut the duplicates: email-readers-tuesday (2nd Tuesday-story after email-the-moment) and email-presence-experience (same destination/CTA as email-try-this-first); fold email-well-be-here into the close
- Add one 'this isn't for everyone' email that names the optimizer and points them to Oura — telling the wrong people no makes the right people feel found
- Add the objection-killer 'a notification wants something from you; this wants nothing' to email-questions-answered, and name the return there
- Trim 11 emails toward ~7 (exact depth unresolved — see dissent)

### TRUE FIT (transactional/sizing)
Keep the receipt-plain restraint, but seed the guarantee and one brand line where doubt first creeps in.

- State the unconditional 30-day return in email-order-confirmed and the sizing emails — fit anxiety converts hardest on a no-questions promise
- Carry one quiet line of the brand into email-order-confirmed's flat hook; it is the single most-opened email you own
- Pull email-gift-of-presence out of the sizing flow into one coherent gifter sub-thread
- Guard any 'report' language from ever becoming a number

### ARRIVAL
Protect the restraint; kill one hype hook; put the return where buyer anxiety peaks.

- Rewrite email-on-its-way's hook 'This is the email you've been waiting for' — it manufactures craving, the opposite of presence
- State the unconditional return once, unmeasured, in email-no-rush / email-day-one — the box just landed and the buyer is most anxious here
- Keep email-its-here CTA-light, but a one-line setup primer can prime activation before the box lands

### FIRST 30 DAYS
The emotional peak of the year — and it re-installs the scoreboard three times. De-score, de-streak, de-duplicate.

- Change email-30-days-present CTA off 'Take the quiz again' to a look-back mirror — re-scoring presence at day 30 betrays the wedge
- Cut email-21-day-threshold — habit/streak framing, no CTA, no job
- Merge the day-3 email-what-did-you-notice ('Reply with your two words' — keep this canon line) and day-4 email-how-is-it-going into one gentle check-in; two asks in two days over-asks a brand that asks nothing back
- De-number the milestone subjects so the month reads as arriving, not leveling up
- Pipe the collected 'two words' back into AWAKENING as human, non-numeric proof

### SUNDAY PAUSE / WIN-BACK
Two opposite jobs stapled together — split them, and never reference wear the ring cannot see.

- Separate Sunday Pause (ongoing opt-in ritual) from Win-Back (time-based re-invite)
- Win-back must never imply 'we noticed you stopped' — the ring reports nothing; that line would betray the whole no-tracking promise
- Lead reactivation with email-one-minute-no-ring (asks for nothing) before email-come-back-to-now
- Give email-come-back-to-now one concrete zero-effort door, kept explicitly guilt-free
- Make email-pause-by-text explicitly opt-in so a text channel never becomes the notification the ring escapes

### RIPPLE (review/referral/gift)
Reciprocity runs backwards and three asks are tangled — give before you ask, and separate the lines.

- Reorder email-what-youve-given (the no-ask mirror) AHEAD of email-give-a-month and email-someone-you-love
- Warm email-someone-you-love off its guilt-poke subject into the quiz's own tender question
- Collapse the scattered gift emails (gift-of-presence, someone-thought-of-you, give-a-month, someone-you-love) into one gifter sub-thread + one recipient email
- Move email-presence-circle out of RIPPLE and use it as the bridge INTO Inner Circle — community is engagement, not referral
- Give the gift path the same 30-day safety net

### INNER CIRCLE (advocates)
Some of the best founder lines in the corpus, buried below the fold, and asks with no button.

- Pull founder gold into subjects: email-shape-whats-next to 'Built with you, not near you'
- Arm every ask with one gentle one-tap CTA (email-tell-your-story, email-we-noticed)
- Sharpen email-we-noticed's cold subject 'We noticed your practice' — a surveillance read that grazes the no-tracking wedge — to the hook's own 'Because you wrote first'
- Soften email-showing-the-way's pedestal framing to what the story actually did
- Fix email-your-story's greeting-card subject
- Keep email-first-hundred identity-scarce ('about a hundred people who shaped this'), never seat- or clock-scarce

### SPACES (studios/corporate)
Strong and restrained, but slow to the lead and one word from collapsing the wedge.

- email-spaces-inquiry: warm the cold auto-reply subject AND drive straight to booking the assessment — B2B speed-to-lead is everything
- Keep 'team's presence report' strictly anonymous before/after words, never a dashboard or number — this is the one flow with budget attached, so the anti-data wedge is most at risk here
- Extend the assessment's restraint ('eight minutes, once — the only homework') across the whole flow; lead with the felt pulse before the proposal

## Strategy roadmap

1. EXCISE THE SCORE SYSTEM-WIDE (all five agree, non-negotiable): rename email-quiz-results off 'Your presence score', change email-30-days-present's CTA off 'Take the quiz again', and cut email-21-day-threshold's streak framing. A ring that proudly keeps no score cannot mail anyone a grade — a coherence pass across flows, not one edit.
2. ADD THE UNCONDITIONAL 30-DAY RETURN to the close and the arrival — email-when-youre-ready, email-still-thinking, email-questions-answered, email-order-confirmed, email-no-rush — framed as the maker's certainty, never a countdown or 'act now'. It is the board's decided offer, in zero of 55 emails, and for a brand that threw away the chart it is the only proof mechanism left standing. On the decisive close, risk reversal is the single biggest unit-mover.
3. CUT THE CLEAR DUPLICATES: email-readers-tuesday, email-presence-experience, merge day-3/day-4 check-ins, cut email-21-day-threshold, fold email-well-be-here into the close. Trim AWAKENING 11 toward ~7 and the system toward the high-30s — a ring that asks nothing back cannot ship inside a calendar that never stops asking.
4. ADD ONE 'THIS ISN'T FOR EVERYONE' EMAIL to AWAKENING (after questions-answered, before the close): name the optimizer who wants streaks and percentiles and gently send them to Oura. Turning the wrong people away makes the right people feel found.
5. SPLIT SUNDAY PAUSE FROM WIN-BACK: Sunday Pause an ongoing opt-in ritual, Win-Back a strictly time-based re-invite. Because the ring reports nothing, win-back can only gently re-offer — never 'we saw you stop.' Lead it with email-one-minute-no-ring, then email-come-back-to-now's 'Try one minute' door.
6. SCRUB LATENT SURVEILLANCE SUBJECTS: 'We noticed your practice' (fixed this pass to 'Because you wrote first') is the tell — audit every subject line for any phrase that implies the ring or the brand watched the wearer, and rewrite to self-report or invitation.
7. REORDER RIPPLE TO GIVE BEFORE IT ASKS: email-what-youve-given ahead of email-give-a-month and email-someone-you-love. Consolidate the scattered gift emails into one gifter sub-thread + one recipient email, and move email-presence-circle out to bridge into Inner Circle.
8. BUILD THE PROOF LOOP: pipe the 'two words' and week-one noticings collected in FIRST 30 DAYS, anonymized, back into pre-purchase emails as the only allowed (human, non-numeric) before/after proof that fills the no-graph hole.
9. ARM THE ADVOCATE FLOW: give every ask one gentle one-tap CTA and lift the buried founder lines into subjects; keep email-first-hundred identity-scarce, never seat- or clock-scarce.

'Waiting' is the board-sanctioned diagnosis — the hours waiting for you — naming the SIZE of the problem, never a grade or percentile. New CTA beats the flat 'See your full result' with quiet curiosity and no number. Keeps the excellent hook 'You answered honestly. Most people don't.'
- **email-30-days-present** — CTA to “Look back at day one” — GUARDRAIL + mandatory: removes 'Take the quiz again', which re-installs the scorecard at the month's emotional peak. A backward mirror, not a re-grade — no re-measurement of presence. Keeps the gorgeous hook intact.
- **email-when-youre-ready** — CTA to “Wear it a month, then decide” — Threads the unconditional 30-day return calmly as the maker's certainty — no deadline, no 'act now'. Resolves the one weak lever (perceived risk) on the decisive close; original CTA 'When you're ready' made no offer at all. Slightly less poetic than the original, but risk-reversal on the close is the single biggest unit-mover, so it stays. ## Where the board still disagrees
Two live disagreements survive both passes. (1) The number question: Hormozi alone wants to SURFACE the reclaimable-hours figure from the quiz as a value anchor for the $200; Watts and Godin want zero numbers anywhere near presence. Sutherland's bridge holds — the quiz may name the SIZE of the problem (hours waiting, off the hero) as diagnosis, but must never hand back a presence score or percentile. This is why email-quiz-results' new subject 'A map of what's waiting' is allowed but 'A map, not a grade' is not: even negating the banned word 'grade' re-introduces the scoreboard on the funnel's first line. (2) Depth of cuts: Jobs pushes 55 to ~38 and Watts pushes AWAKENING to ~8, deeper than Hormozi. The board agrees unanimously on the named duplicate cuts but leaves the final total unsettled — start with the agreed duplicates, measure, then decide. PASS 2 RESULT: all 13 edits verified against the live corpus (every claimed original matched) and all clear the anti-data / no-hype / banned-words check. One sharpening applied — email-we-noticed gains a subject fix ('Because you wrote first') because the untouched original 'We noticed your practice' reads as surveillance on a cold open; no board member's stated guardrail objects, and it strictly improves clarity. No edit was dropped: each genuinely beats its original. Weakest kept edit is email-when-youre-ready's CTA (less poetic than the original) — retained because risk reversal on the close outweighs the polish loss.

## Each advisor's biggest win

- **Alex Hormozi** — Put the risk reversal into the sequence. The board already decided the offer, an unconditional 30-day return that grades nothing, and it appears in none of the 55 emails, while AWAKENING makes no clean offer or buy ask across 11 emails. Thread that guarantee through the close (when-youre-ready, still-thinking) and seed it in order-confirmed. It directly shrinks your one weak value-equation lever (perceived likelihood), costs nothing in returns for a product this sticky, and does all of it without a single exclamation point, countdown, or banned word. You wrote a world-class nurture and forgot to make the yes obvious; this makes it obvious.
- **Seth Godin** — Kill the presence 'score' everywhere and reframe it as a map. It's the one change that touches the whole system and protects the whole system. The score currently opens your very first email ('Your presence score') and reappears at the emotional peak of month one ('take the quiz again') — so the two moments a customer most decides what story to tell themselves about Pulse are the two moments you hand them a grade. But 'I own the one thing that keeps no score of me' is the flag that makes this remarkable; 'I got a 72 on the presence test' files it next to every wearable it's trying to escape. Every advisor on the board named this as the fatal crack, and they're right. Reframe the quiz result as a map of what's waiting ('That's not a grade — it's a map,' already in your own corpus), make the day-30 moment a look-back mirror instead of a re-test, and the anti-data soul stays intact across all eight flows. Nail that, and the tribe has something worth waving.
- **Alan Watts** — Rip the scorecard out of the lifecycle entirely. It is sneaking back in three places — 'Your presence score' (quiz-results), 'Take the quiz again' (30-days-present), and 'The 21-day threshold' (a streak in disguise). Each one turns a homecoming into homework, and the moment a ring that 'knows nothing about you, on purpose' mails you a grade, the entire anti-data soul collapses and it's simply Fitbit in gold. Remove all three and the brand's deepest promise — nothing to check, ever, nothing to pass or fail — finally holds all the way through the letters, not just in the hero line.
- **Steve Jobs** — The cadence has to become the product. A ring whose entire promise is 'nothing to check, asks nothing back' cannot ship inside 55 emails with eight check-ins in the first 30 days — the medium is calling the message a liar. Cut roughly a third of the sequence, starting with the two duplicate Tuesday stories, the two duplicate Presence-Experience emails, the double day-3/day-4 check-in, and the habit-framed 21-day email. Then take the room you just freed and put the two things that are actually missing into it: the unconditional 'send it back, no reason needed' return, and the one-line kill of 'isn't this just another buzz?' Say no to a thousand emails so the ones that remain are inevitable.
- **Rory Sutherland** — The unconditional return, reframed as proof rather than protection. Add 'Wear it a month; if it isn't for you, send it back, no reason, no questions' to the purchase-decision emails (when-youre-ready, still-thinking, questions-answered) and the sizing flow, but sell it as the maker's certainty, not a refund policy. Here is the counterintuitive part: a brand that has deliberately refused the chart, the score and the graph has thrown away its usual proof mechanism. A no-questions guarantee does the graph's job better than the graph ever could, because it is a costly signal that says the maker is sure, and it lets the buyer stop calculating and simply try. It shrinks the one genuinely weak lever ('but will it work for ME?') to near zero without a single number, urgency cue, scarcity claim or exclamation point. The board wrote the line in ten passes of consensus; the flows never once said it out loud. Say it.
