# Pulse Visual Language — "The Movie of Your Life" (the film-strip device)

A core storytelling device and style transition for the Pulse visual system.
Live prototype: `pulse-of-life.html`.

## The idea

Your life is a movie — twenty-four frames a second. The question the ring asks
is whether you're living it at the speed you can actually see.

- **Autopilot = a film strip running too fast.** Every frame a *different* event
  (commute, inbox, dishes, years), flickering past, cold and desaturated, none
  of them seen. This *is* the "days that blur" — literally a strip moving faster
  than the eye. Grammar: many distinct frames, fast horizontal race, grayscale +
  under-exposed, subtle flicker, cool cast.
- **The pulse = the decelerator.** The ring's tap is what slows the reel. Shown
  as the brand's concentric pulse-ring beat; the film-strip animation duration
  stretches on cue (5.5s → 38s).
- **Presence = 24 frames of ONE moment.** The strip slows until twenty-four
  frames hold a *single* instant — the same moment, fully lived, seamless. Not
  more footage; the same footage at the speed of being here. Grammar: the *same*
  frame repeated across the strip, warming from grey to honey gold, then the
  center frame "comes alive" as a seamless motion loop (our existing b-roll).
- **Payoff:** "Make the most of the movie of your life."

## Why it works

- It restates the whole brand arc (numb → present) in one visual grammar.
- The film metaphor is honest to the product: a *pulse* is a frame rate; presence
  is choosing the rate. "Same footage, slower" is a truer promise than "new life."
- It reuses the two-worlds palette: the cold/fast strip is the numb world, the
  warm/slow held moment is the present world. The transition between them IS the
  temperature change we already use on the arrival-day email.

## Style-transition rules (reusable anywhere)

1. **Different-frames → same-frame** is the signature move. Any sequence of
   distinct thumbnails that resolves into one repeated held frame reads as
   "autopilot → presence." Use for section transitions, video wipes, loaders.
2. **Cold + fast → warm + slow.** Desaturate and speed up the "before"; warm and
   slow the "after." Never the reverse.
3. **The pulse is always the hinge.** The concentric-ring beat is what causes the
   slowdown — motion must originate from it, not just happen.
4. **One held moment, not a montage.** The resolution is always a *single*
   moment, seamless, long enough to become a movie. Resist cutting.
5. **Sprocket holes are the only ornament.** Film grammar stays minimal —
   perforations top/bottom, thin frame dividers, nothing else.
6. **Motion earns its place.** If a strip animation doesn't carry the numb→present
   story, cut it. This is a story device first, a decoration never.

## To generate later (needs image budget)

The prototype uses existing stills/loops. The device gets dramatically stronger
with a purpose-shot **"same moment × 24" sequence**: 24 near-identical frames of
one instant (e.g. Maya's face crossing one small realization), rendered as a true
24-frame micro-loop — so the "held moment" is literally 24 frames of one second,
not a Ken-Burns still. Queue: `tools/` sequence generator, aspect 3:2, one
character-locked moment, micro-variation only. Also a cold "autopilot montage"
of 24 genuinely different 1-second frames for the race strip.
