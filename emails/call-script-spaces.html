The Decision Call

25 minutes · their space on the calendar, not ours

Goal

Leave the call with a shaped pilot: which space or team, how many rings, and a sizing-day date on the calendar. Not a signed contract — a shape. If they need time, leave with a follow-up date instead. Both are wins. Pressure is the only loss.

Opening
"Thanks for the time, {{first_name}}. I've read your assessment. Before I say anything — what does two in the afternoon feel like at {{company_name}}?"

Then be quiet and listen. Their answer is the call. Everything you propose from here gets tied back to what they just described.

Three beats
  1. Their space's rhythm. Mirror their words back. Ask when the day is loudest, and when people are physically there but somewhere else in their heads. You're finding the hour the pilot will serve — let them name it.
  2. The pilot shape. One team or one floor. {{ring_count}} rings, one per person, each sized to its own hand. The shared quiet hour they chose. One month, then the team report — the group's own before-and-after words, no names.
  3. Sizing day logistics. Pick the date on the call. One afternoon covers forty people — five minutes each. We bring the sizers and run the table; they provide a quiet corner and tell people when to drop by.
What NOT to say
  • Never "ROI on mindfulness." If they ask for it, the honest answer: the measurable story is the team's own words, before and after, anonymous. That's the only number we'd trust, so it's the only one we sell.
  • Never individual data. The ring reports nothing and no one is watched. If they ask for per-person reporting, the answer is a calm no — that promise to the wearer is the reason the ring works at all.
  • No promised feelings. "Your team may notice," never "your team will feel." No urgency, no expiring pricing, no exclamation energy.
Close

If they're ready: "I'll send the launch kit today and confirm the sizing day for {{sizing_day_date}}." If they're not: "Take the time you need. I'll write on {{followup_date}} — nothing moves without you." Either way, end one minute early. Calm is the brand, on calls too.
