Inner Circle · Story Call

Can we tell your story?

10–15 minutes · Phone · Follows the story email by 1–2 days

Goal

One clear answer — yes, no, or maybe — freely given. A comfortable no is a success. The relationship is worth more than the footage. Never persuade past the first hesitation.

Opening line
"Hi {{first_name}}, it's Johan from Pulse. Is now still an okay time? I have one question, and it comes with a built-in no."

Then stop talking. Let them settle. If the timing is bad, offer to call back and actually call back.

Three beats
  1. Their moment. Start with what they already told us: {{their_moment}}. Ask them to tell it again in their own voice. Listen. Don't paraphrase it back at them.
  2. What changed for others when they shared. One concrete example: a stranger read a story like theirs and wrote back saying it was the first time they'd felt understood. Their words reach people ours can't.
  3. Logistics. Forty-five minutes. Their kitchen, or wherever the practice lives. One person with a camera — no makeup crew, no script. They see and approve everything before it goes anywhere.
What NOT to say
  • Never say "content." It's their story, not our material.
  • Never say "leverage." Or "asset," "exposure," or "opportunity."
  • Never promise how it will feel. "You may find it enjoyable," not "you'll love it."
  • Never fill their silence. The pause is where the real answer lives.
Close

Thank them the same way for any answer. Confirm the next step out loud: a filming date, a think-it-over window, or a clean no. "Either way, you'll get a short email from me by {{follow_up_day}}. Nothing changes between us."
