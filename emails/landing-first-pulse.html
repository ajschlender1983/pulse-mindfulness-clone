Your first pulse

This page asks for ninety seconds and nothing else. No account, no sound, no timer. Read slowly. That's the only instruction.

Begin
the dot breathes every eight seconds. you can too.
01 · arrive

Sit down. Anywhere is fine.

Both feet on the floor. Let the chair hold your weight — it's been doing it all along, you just haven't noticed lately.

For the next minute and a half, there is nowhere else you need to be. That's not a metaphor. Check: nowhere.

02 · the ring

Put it on.

Whichever hand feels natural. Slide it past the knuckle until it sits snug and quiet. Notice its small weight — that's the whole interface. There is nothing to switch on and nothing to open.

03 · three breaths

Breathe with the dot.

Scroll back up to it if you like, or just remember its pace. In as it grows. Out as it softens. Three times, no more.

If your mind wanders during breath two, that's not failing. Noticing the wandering is the practice working.

04 · the first pulse

It will come when it comes.

Sometime in the next while — maybe in ten minutes, maybe in forty — the ring will hum gently against your finger. You don't need to wait for it. Go make the coffee. Fold the laundry. Live.

When it does come, do one thing: notice where you are. The room, the light, the sound of it. That's all a pulse ever asks.

That's the whole practice.

Nothing was measured just now. No streak began, and none will. You sat down in a room, and for ninety seconds, you were actually in it. The ring will bring you back a few times an hour. The coming back is yours.
