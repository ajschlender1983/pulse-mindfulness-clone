Six weeks in. This invitation waits for that, on purpose — it means something different now than it would have on day two.

The Presence Circle is a quieter corner of the internet. A small room of people who practice the way you do: imperfectly, at kitchen sinks and school pickups, without an audience.

What it is: once a month, a circle call. One question, cameras optional, plenty of silence — the good kind. Between calls, a slow thread where someone occasionally writes what they noticed that week.

What it isn't: a feed. There's nothing to perform, nothing to keep up with, and no one takes attendance. You may go months just reading. That counts as being there.

Ten seconds of quiet, right now.
That's all the Circle is — with company.
