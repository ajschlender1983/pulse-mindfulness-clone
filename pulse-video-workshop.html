One visual system · three cuts
Every frame is one of three media — and every film turns on the same hinge.

The three trailers wear different genres, but they speak one visual language. A shot is either a photograph (the lived present), an illustration (the remembered, the mythic), or the turn — a hand-painted illustration resolving into a warm photograph, timed to the pulse. That resolve is the whole brand in a single frame: autopilot becoming presence. Each frame below is tagged with its medium; the hinge of each film is marked.

Production rules

Every film obeys the motion rules: photographic shots are handheld cell-phone; any illustrated beat is multiplane; a medium change is earned only at the hinge. Homage means trope, never copy — original lines in the Pulse voice, familiar shapes, our world. Masters cut 16:9 with 9:16 crop-safe framing; hooks land inside the first three seconds; captions burned for sound-off viewing. No urgency, no exclamation points — even the anti-ad stays calm.
