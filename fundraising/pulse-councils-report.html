Board 02 · The Brand Council — storytelling
Hormozi — offer & value equation Godin — remarkable & the tribe Watts — presence & meaning Jobs — simplicity & the experience Sutherland — perceived value
Hormozi offer & value equation

The investor deck has no offer for the investor. Founders forget investors buy too. What's the guaranteed-ish outcome, the risk-reversal, the "here's exactly what your money buys and by when"? Your use-of-proceeds slide is a citron box — that is the offer. Make it concrete: "$X buys these four milestones in 18 months, and here's the one that unlocks the A." And on the product: your consumer offer (one-month unconditional return) is gorgeous — steal that energy for the raise.

Godin remarkable & the tribe

The remarkable thing is "the ring that refuses to measure you" — and the fundraising deck almost talks itself out of it by leaning into stats and platform TAM. That's fine for investors, but don't lose the purple cow: the reason 4,000 people paid is the refusal, not the roadmap. Put the refusal back at the emotional center, then let the platform be how it scales. Also: "every brand where you lie down is a partner" is a genuinely remarkable line — that's your headline, not a slide-14 subhead.

Watts presence & meaning

Careful. The moment Pulse becomes a "biometrics platform that reads your brainwaves," it risks handing the wearer back the very scoreboard the ring freed them from. The Pillow slide says "still asks nothing of you" — good, protect that with your life. The deep story is that Pulse measures so you don't have to — the data serves the calm, it isn't the point. Say that, or the soul quietly leaks out through the EEG.

Jobs simplicity & the experience

Two decks, one sentence each. The product deck has it: "a pulse, not a ping." The raise deck doesn't — "raising ahead of the sell-through" is a mechanic, not a meaning. What is this company in one line? I'd argue it's "the calm layer for the whole wellness world." Lead with that, delete a third of the words on the busy slides, and let the traction and the platform be the proof beneath one idea, not four competing ones.

Sutherland perceived value

The "anti-data" stance is your pricing power — don't let the investor deck apologize for it with a wall of numbers. Reframe the EEG not as "more data" but as the one measurement that finally buys you the right to stop measuring — the Pillow reads you so you never have to check. That reframe justifies the platform to investors and keeps the brand intact. And "raising ahead" is a status signal — you're not desperate, you're early. Lean into that; it makes the round feel scarce.

Council agreement: the raise deck is strong on proof but has drifted from the one idea that makes Pulse remarkable — the refusal to measure. Put the soul back at the center and let the platform be how the calm scales, not a competing story.

  2. Reframe the EEG as permission, not data (Sutherland/Watts): "the one measurement that buys you the right to stop measuring." Add it to the Pillow slide.
  3. Promote the partnership line (Godin): "every brand where you lie down becomes a partner" earns a bigger moment.
  4. Make use-of-proceeds a real offer (Hormozi): $X → four dated milestones → the one that unlocks the next round.
  5. Protect the refusal (all): keep "still asks nothing of you" as a load-bearing promise, not a reassurance.
