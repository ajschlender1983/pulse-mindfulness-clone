Act I · the churn

We are all here. And we are all somewhere else.

Every "before" beat now has its own reference still — the father on his phone, the couple back-to-back in bed, the quick-cut triptych, the crosswalk crowd, the building at dusk — before the frame becomes a painting.

The mind movie

It was never only the phone.

Two people, empty hands, still gone — stuck in the movie of the mind instead of making the movie of their lives. A small ghostly film-frame beside their head makes it literal. The camera goes still and quiet here — the opposite of the crowd churn's frantic search — because the restlessness lives inside the little screen, not the lens.
